[Bullet Points]
- Objective 1: A Multiphysics Simulation Framework from Radiation Damage to Device Degradation
	- Module 1: Geant4 radiation transport
	- Module 2: Electrostatics
	- Module 3: Defect diffusion and annealing
	- Module 4: Thermal transport
	- Module 5: Charge carrier transport
	- Module 6: Multiphysics coupling: Modules 2 - 5

	- Importance quantification and ranking of each module
	- Stepwise reduction of simulation with minimum loss of simulation fidelity
	- Extrapolation back to full-scale interpretation.


[Script]
- The physics I am trying to simulate is the pathway from radiation energy deposition on top of the device to device degradation in silicon. 
- A radiation event first creates a localized disturbance in the material through ionization, energy deposition, or even knocking out atoms at the lattice sites, initiating the defect generation. 
- Those defects do not remain static; they diffuse, anneal, react, and modify the local electrical and thermal environment of the device. 
- In a full-physics model, each stage of this chain can be represented by coupled transport, defect-kinematics, electrostatic, thermal, and carrier-transport equations. 
- The central challenge is to identify which parts of this chain dominate the final device response, so that the model can be reduced efficiently while preserving the physics that matters most.
